Blue Lock vs Hunter x Hunter – Which Manga Should You Read First?

📅 June 2026 ⏱ 8 min read 🏷 Comparison · Blue Lock · Hunter x Hunter

Two of the most discussed manga in 2026 — but they serve completely different reading experiences. Blue Lock launched in 2018 and rapidly became one of the most-read sports manga globally, built around a radical football program where individual ego overrides teamwork. Hunter x Hunter has been running since 1998 and returns in June 2026 with Chapter 411, carrying a reputation as one of the most strategically complex and emotionally layered manga ever written.

This comparison is based on reading both series in full. It covers story structure, character writing, power systems, tone, pacing, and a clear verdict on which series to pick up first depending on what you are looking for as a reader.

📌 Quick answer: Blue Lock if you want fast, competitive energy. Hunter x Hunter if you want depth, complexity, and one of manga's greatest long-form narratives. Both are worth your time — and this guide will help you decide where to start.

Story — Structure and Direction

Blue Lock

Blue Lock has one of the most focused premises in recent sports manga. Japan sets up a radical football program to manufacture the world's best striker, run by Jinpachi Ego, who believes ego and individual dominance matter more than teamwork. Every arc builds directly on the one before it, raising the level of competition as the players evolve. The story never loses sight of its core argument — that a single exceptional striker can change the outcome of any match.

This tight focus makes Blue Lock extremely easy to follow. You always know what the stakes are and what each character is fighting for. The pacing is relentless, and nearly every chapter ends with the kind of tension that makes it hard to stop reading.

Hunter x Hunter

Hunter x Hunter operates on a completely different scale. It begins as a classic adventure — a boy looking for his missing father — but expands into something far more ambitious with each new arc. The series changes genre almost entirely between storylines, moving from adventure to crime thriller to war epic to political drama without losing the thread of its core characters.

This variety is what makes HxH so celebrated, but it also means the series demands more patience. Some arcs are dense and deliberately paced. The payoffs are enormous, but the journey to get there requires real investment from the reader.

Characters — Who Stands Out More

Blue Lock's Cast

Every character in Blue Lock exists to challenge Isagi in a specific way. Rin Itoshi represents technical perfection. Michael Kaiser represents global-level confidence. The cast is built to function as both rivals and mirrors, each one forcing Isagi to evolve or be eliminated. Character development in Blue Lock is fast, visible, and tied directly to match performance.

Hunter x Hunter's Cast

Hunter x Hunter has one of the deepest ensemble casts in manga. Gon, Killua, Kurapika, and Leorio each carry their own fully realized storyline with personal stakes that run across the entire series. Even antagonists like Meruem and Chrollo Lucilfer are given enough depth that their worldviews feel genuinely considered rather than simply villainous. The cast rewards long-term investment in a way that few series match.

Power System — Which Is More Interesting

Blue Lock — Spatial Awareness and Football Instinct

Blue Lock's version of a power system is entirely grounded in football. Isagi's spatial awareness, Rin's technical precision, Kaiser's offensive confidence — these are real football attributes elevated to near-supernatural levels through the intensity of the program. There are no magic abilities, but the series treats football intelligence as something that can be developed and evolved in the same way a power system would be.

This grounded approach makes the competitive scenes feel genuinely exciting rather than arbitrary, because the rules of what each player can and cannot do stay consistent throughout.

Hunter x Hunter — The Nen System

Nen is widely considered one of the best-designed power systems in all of manga. It operates on a clear internal logic — six categories, each with its own strengths and costs, plus advanced mechanics like Vows and Limitations that reward users who impose constraints on themselves. High-level Nen battles play out like strategic arguments, where the outcome depends on preparation, knowledge, and creativity rather than raw power.

The Nen system is one of the primary reasons Hunter x Hunter maintains such strong critical standing decades after it started. It never stops being interesting because Togashi keeps finding new ways to apply it.

Tone and Difficulty — What Kind of Reader Are You

Blue Lock is immediate and energetic. It rewards readers who want fast progression, clear stakes, and a story that never slows down. You can pick it up, read ten chapters, and feel like a lot has happened — because it has.

Hunter x Hunter is slower and more demanding. Some of its arcs — particularly the Chimera Ant arc and the current Succession Contest — require careful attention because Togashi layers strategy, character psychology, and world politics simultaneously. The reward for that attention is immense, but it is a series that asks more from its readers.

Final Verdict — Which Should You Read First

🏆 Read This First Based on What You Want

Blue Lock First

If you want fast pacing, clear competitive stakes, football-driven tension, and a story where every chapter moves the needle. Perfect if you are newer to manga or want something immediately engaging.

HxH First

If you want the deepest, most strategically layered manga experience available. Ideal if you are willing to invest in a longer read with slower arcs that pay off in a major way.

Read Both

Honestly, the best approach. Start with Blue Lock for instant momentum, then move into Hunter x Hunter once you want something with greater scope and complexity. The two series complement each other well because they are so different.
